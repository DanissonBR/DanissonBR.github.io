Ender 3 Repair & 3D Printing

Restored a broken Ender 3 through hardware troubleshooting, electrical diagnostics and component-level analysis.

3D Printing Troubleshooting Oscilloscope Stepper Motors Hardware Repair
View details

This project started with a broken Ender 3 3D printer that I received for repair and experimentation. The printer had axis movement failures, so I began by isolating the possible causes.

I tested the stepper motors to verify if they were still functional, checked whether the power supply was delivering enough energy and analyzed the motion control signals with an oscilloscope.

After testing the motors and the power supply, I inspected the STEP signals for the motion drivers. The oscilloscope showed abnormal behavior on the X and Y axis signals, which led me to conclude that the X and Y stepper drivers were damaged.

After the repair process, the project evolved into print calibration, quality improvement and 3D printing workflow experiments.

Digital Logic, Counter & ADC Display

Breadboard-based electronics setup combining a digital lock, counter logic and an 8-bit ADC display system.

Digital Logic CD4017 CD4093 CD4510 8-bit ADC LCD
View details

This setup combines three electronics projects into a single prototyping board. The first project is a digital lock based on a CD4017 sequencer.

The second project is a 0–9 counter using a 4093 logic IC as the clock source and a CD4510 counter. The third project is an 8-bit ADC reading system displayed through an LCD.

In this setup, the Arduino is not used as the main logic controller. It is mainly used for clock support and to translate the ADC output into readable information on the LCD display. Most of the logic is implemented with discrete digital ICs.
